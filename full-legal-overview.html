Legal Framework of Surrogacy in Armenia

Armenia provides a defined legal framework that permits surrogacy arrangements under regulated conditions.

When properly structured, surrogacy programs can be carried out with legal clarity, including the establishment of parental rights and issuance of birth documentation.

VitaBridge Consulting coordinates each stage in alignment with applicable legal and procedural requirements.

Is Surrogacy Legal in Armenia?

Yes. Surrogacy is permitted under Armenian law when conducted through proper agreements and medical procedures.

The process involves:

  • Formal agreements between parties
  • Licensed medical involvement
  • Proper documentation and registration

Who Can Participate

Surrogacy programs in Armenia are available to:

  • Heterosexual couples
  • Single intended parents (subject to legal and procedural considerations)

Eligibility may vary depending on:

  • Nationality
  • Marital status
  • Medical indications

Each case is reviewed individually to ensure compliance with applicable regulations.

How the Process Is Structured

A surrogacy arrangement typically includes:

  • Legal agreements between intended parents and surrogate
  • Medical procedures carried out by licensed clinics
  • Documentation governing parental rights
  • Birth registration procedures

All documents must be properly prepared and aligned with Armenian legal requirements.

Establishing Legal Parenthood

In a properly structured program:

  • Intended parents are recognized as legal parents
  • The surrogate does not retain parental rights after birth (subject to legal framework and agreements)
  • Parentage is reflected in official birth documentation
  • The birth certificate will include only the name of the intended single parent and no reference to the surrogate will be made

Legal procedures ensure that parental rights are established in accordance with applicable laws.

Birth Certificate and Documentation

Following delivery:

  • Birth is registered with relevant authorities
  • Birth certificate is issued reflecting intended parents
  • Additional documentation may be required for international use

For International Intended Parents

Foreign intended parents can participate in surrogacy programs in Armenia, subject to:

  • Compliance with Armenian legal procedures
  • Documentation requirements
  • Coordination with their home country regulations

Exit procedures typically include:

  • Passport application for the child
  • Embassy interaction
  • Travel clearance

Process Integrity

To ensure a legally compliant process:

  • All agreements must be properly drafted
  • Medical procedures must be conducted through licensed providers
  • Documentation must be accurate and complete
  • Each stage must be coordinated professionally

Individual Case Review

Each surrogacy case is unique.

Legal, medical, and administrative requirements may vary based on:

  • Country of origin
  • Family structure
  • Medical factors

For this reason, every program should be structured individually.
