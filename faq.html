Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common questions about the surrogacy process in Armenia.

General Questions

Surrogacy is a structured medical and legal process in which a surrogate carries a pregnancy on behalf of intended parents. The process includes medical procedures, legal agreements, and coordinated case management — from initial consultation to birth and documentation.

Eligibility depends on medical, legal, and individual factors. Each case is reviewed individually to determine the appropriate program structure.

Programs are structured based on medical requirements, process complexity, and desired level of coordination. A recommendation is provided following an initial case review.

Legal Questions

Surrogacy is regulated within the framework of applicable laws governing assisted reproductive technologies. Each case is structured in accordance with these legal provisions. Learn more about the legal framework in Armenia.

Parental rights are established through defined legal procedures and documentation processes aligned with applicable regulations.

Legal documentation is prepared and coordinated with experienced Armenian legal professionals specialized in surrogacy-related matters.
