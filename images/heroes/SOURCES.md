## Why each pick

- **`index-hero.jpg`** — Two adult hands held together at golden-hour sunset against a soft field background. Gender is genuinely unreadable from the sleeves/skin — no jewelry, nails, or skin-tone tells. Editorial, emotional, and broadly inclusive — a same-sex, opposite-sex, or single-parent visitor can all see themselves in it. Soft natural light, no faces.
- **`about-hero.jpg`** *(2026-05-16 refresh — warmer family-bonding direction)* — Adult hand cradling a newborn's feet. Intimate close-crop, no faces, warm intimate tones. Replaces the earlier "two people at a consultation table" shot, which a reviewer felt was too neutral to communicate surrogacy at all. The hand-and-baby-feet read leans into the emotional outcome the brand is selling.
- **`programs-hero.jpg`** *(2026-05-16 refresh — nursery still life)* — Folded baby clothes arranged in a woven basket. Neutral cream/sage palette, no people, soft natural light. Implies "preparing for a child" without showing a child — fits the "Programs" page where intended parents are weighing structured options for a journey that ends with this kind of moment.
- **`process-hero.jpg`** — Curving stone path through grass. Journey metaphor, no people, calm and forward-moving. Used on the protected account page; also originally used on `process.html` but `process.html` now uses `process-journey-hero.jpg` instead.
- **`process-journey-hero.jpg`** *(2026-05-16 — same-sex / inclusivity slot, with caveat)* — Faceless silhouette of an adult carrying a baby at golden hour. The brief called for a same-sex couple, faceless, warm; stock-photo reality is that *same-sex parent shots with babies all prioritize visible faces* (Pexels' 10,000+ "gay parents" tag has zero silhouette/back-turned options). The compromise: a single-adult silhouette that's gender-ambiguous and warm — readable as a parent of any kind. Inclusivity in copy/content on this page should compensate for what the image alone can't say.
- **`legal-hero.jpg`** *(2026-05-16 — new file for full-legal-overview)* — Stack of papers on a warm wooden table. Replaces the previous arrangement where `full-legal-overview.html` shared `why-armenia-hero.jpg` (a geographic landscape) — a semantic mismatch. Pure paperwork on warm wood; the "paperwork-meets-family" composition (documents + baby blanket together) effectively doesn't exist as a single stock shot, so this pick stays clean rather than forcing a contrived combination.
- **`why-armenia-hero.jpg`** *(2026-05-28 refresh — color Armenia landmark)* — Khor Virap monastery in the foreground (golden stone on a green plain with vineyards and a hay wagon) and snow-capped Mount Ararat dominating the background. Iconic Armenia composition. Replaces the previous black-and-white Zvartnots-with-Ararat shot, which violated the brand rule against black-and-white imagery; sage greens and golden stone now align with the site's sage+gold palette.
- **`faq-hero.jpg`** *(2026-05-26 refresh — sage journal on a styled desk)* — Sage-green leather diary embossed "Writing for Wholly Town Charter", lit votive candle in a textured glass holder, daffodils in a fluted glass vase, multi-coloured bookmark tabs, all on a sage-striped placemat. Strongest on-palette pick in this pass — the diary itself sits in the brand's sage range and the daffodils add the warm-gold accent without competing with the typography. Replaces the Noha Badawi notebook+candle shot.
- **`insights-hero.jpg`** *(2026-05-16 refresh — warm-but-restrained reading)* — Opened book on white linen textile, warm natural tones. Used across the insights hub and all 5 article pages, so deliberately kept restrained — no baby/family cues that would feel forced on pure-info article pages.
- **`couple-hero.jpg`** *(2026-05-28 refresh — less intimate, smiling, on-palette)* — Two men walking hand-in-hand through a sunlit tree-lined park, both smiling at each other, golden-hour backlight through the foliage. Cropped from the original 2400×3600 portrait to 2400×1600 landscape so the full-bleed `hero-image` break renders without losing the subjects' faces. Keeps the deliberate same-sex / inclusivity intent of the previous image (per [[process-journey-hero]] caveat) but trades the intimate close-up of hands+rings for a warmer, more reserved "walking together" framing, per a brief asking for less intimacy and visible warmth. Green park palette aligns with the site's sage tones.

## Notes

- All files downloaded at width 1920 px (process at 1400, insights at 1600) — large enough for full-bleed hero use on retina screens, small enough for GitHub Pages without Git LFS.
- `process-hero.jpg` is ~773 KB even after recompressing — high-detail nature shot, doesn't compress further without visible artifacts. Still well under the GitHub 100 MB hard limit.
- If you later want a `<picture>` element with mobile fallbacks, re-fetch each Unsplash URL with `w=800` and save as e.g. `index-hero-800.jpg`.
- To re-download an Unsplash image at a different size: take its `images.unsplash.com/photo-<id>` URL and change `w=` and `q=` params. The numeric photo ID is in the og:image meta tag on each photo page.
- To re-download a Pexels image at a different size: `https://images.pexels.com/photos/<id>/pexels-photo-<id>.jpeg?auto=compress&cs=tinysrgb&w=<width>`. Some older Pexels assets (pre-2017 IDs like `38302`) use a slug-based filename — `pexels-photo-<id>.jpeg` will 404; use the form `https://images.pexels.com/photos/<id>/<slug>-<id>.jpeg` from the photo page's download link instead.
- The 2026-05-26 image swap (`hero-mother-child`, `why-armenia-landing-man-child`, `faq-hero`, plus the new `consultation-hero` and `eligibility-hero`) was picked under "change the images however u prefer" — the reverse-image-search uniqueness step from the project's `feedback_image_sourcing` convention was deferred. Worth a 5-minute Google Lens / TinEye pass on each before publishing to GitHub Pages.
