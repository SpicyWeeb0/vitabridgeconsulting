Surrogacy Process Step-by-Step in Armenia

The surrogacy process in Armenia is a structured journey that combines medical procedures, legal coordination, and process management.

Understanding each stage helps intended parents prepare for the journey and ensures that the process is approached with clarity and confidence.

When properly organized, each step is aligned within a coordinated framework, allowing the process to progress smoothly from initial consultation to birth and documentation.

Overview of the Process

Surrogacy is not a single event but a sequence of interconnected stages.

Each stage must be properly coordinated to ensure:

  • Continuity between medical and legal steps
  • Proper timing of procedures
  • Alignment between all involved parties

Below is a structured overview of how the process typically unfolds.

Step 1 — Initial Consultation and Case Review

The process begins with an initial assessment of the intended parents' situation.

This includes:

  • Medical background review
  • Discussion of goals and expectations
  • Preliminary evaluation of program suitability

This stage defines the foundation of the entire journey.

Step 2 — Program Structuring

Based on the initial assessment, the appropriate program is selected.

This stage includes:

  • Defining the structure of the process
  • Planning medical and legal steps
  • Establishing the overall coordination framework

A clear structure ensures that the process progresses in a controlled and predictable manner. Learn more in our article on choosing the right surrogacy program.

Step 3 — Medical Preparation

Medical preparation involves IVF procedures and embryo development.

This stage includes:

  • Fertility treatment and embryo creation
  • Medical planning for embryo transfer
  • Coordination with the medical team

All procedures are scheduled and aligned with the overall process plan.

Step 4 — Surrogate And Donor Matching

A suitable surrogate is selected through a structured process.

This includes:

  • Medical and background screening
  • Matching based on program requirements
  • Preparation for participation in the process

The matching stage is critical for ensuring compatibility and process stability.

Step 5 — Legal Structuring

Legal agreements and documentation are prepared before the medical procedure continues.

This stage includes:

  • Drafting and signing agreements
  • Defining roles and responsibilities
  • Preparing documentation required for the process

Legal structuring ensures clarity and alignment between all parties. See the full legal framework article for more detail.

Step 6 — Embryo Transfer

The embryo transfer procedure is performed following medical and legal preparation.

This stage includes:

  • Scheduling and performing the transfer
  • Medical monitoring after the procedure
  • Confirmation of pregnancy

This is a key milestone in the process.

Step 7 — Pregnancy Monitoring

Following a successful transfer, the pregnancy is monitored throughout its duration.

This includes:

  • Regular medical check-ups
  • Ongoing coordination and supervision
  • Alignment with planned process stages

This stage ensures continuity and stability throughout the pregnancy.

Step 8 — Birth and Documentation

The final stage involves the birth of the child and completion of legal procedures.

This includes:

  • Organization of delivery
  • Completion of required documentation
  • Formal establishment of parental rights

This stage finalizes the process and ensures proper legal recognition.

Process Coordination

Each of these stages is interconnected.

Effective coordination ensures:

  • Proper sequencing of steps
  • Alignment between medical and legal components
  • Continuity throughout the entire journey

Without structured coordination, the process can become fragmented.

Timeline Considerations

The duration of the process varies depending on individual factors.

However, with structured planning, the timeline is managed to:

  • Reduce unnecessary delays
  • Maintain continuity between stages
  • Ensure efficient progression

Each case is planned individually to reflect its specific requirements. For a closer look at the factors affecting duration, see our timeline article.

Conclusion

The surrogacy process in Armenia is a structured journey that requires coordination across multiple stages.

Understanding how the process works allows intended parents to approach it with clarity and confidence. For the bigger picture, read our main overview.
