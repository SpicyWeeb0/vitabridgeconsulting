How to Choose the Right Surrogacy Program in Armenia

Choosing the right surrogacy program is one of the most important decisions in the process.

In Armenia, programs are structured to reflect different medical situations, process requirements, and levels of coordination.

Understanding how programs differ allows intended parents to select an approach that aligns with their specific case and expectations.

Why Program Selection Matters

Surrogacy is not a standardized process.

Each journey depends on multiple factors, including medical conditions, previous history, and overall process complexity.

Selecting the appropriate program helps:

  • Align the process with medical requirements
  • Ensure continuity across stages
  • Reduce the need for restructuring during the journey
  • Maintain control over the process

Key Factors to Consider

1. Medical Situation

Medical factors play a central role in program selection.

This may include:

  • Fertility history
  • Embryo availability and quality
  • Need for additional procedures

Programs are structured to reflect these conditions and provide an appropriate level of support.

2. Number of Required Attempts

Some cases may require more than one embryo transfer.

Programs differ in how they structure this aspect of the process. A well-selected program allows:

  • Continuity without restarting the process
  • Flexibility in managing multiple attempts

3. Level of Process Coordination

The level of coordination varies between programs.

Factors include:

  • Degree of supervision and case management
  • Alignment between medical and legal stages
  • Level of involvement throughout the process

Higher levels of coordination provide greater control and continuity.

4. Complexity of the Case

Some cases require a more advanced structure.

This may involve:

  • Additional medical planning
  • Enhanced coordination
  • More complex process management

Programs are designed to accommodate different levels of complexity.

5. Planning Objectives

The intended outcome also influences program selection.

For example:

  • Single or dual surrogacy journeys
  • Desired timeline
  • Level of predictability and continuity

These objectives should be aligned with the program structure.

Types of Program Structures

While programs vary, they generally fall into structured categories:

  • Standard structured programs for balanced and straightforward cases
  • Continuity-based programs designed to allow ongoing progression
  • Advanced programs with enhanced medical and coordination strategies
  • Parallel programs for dual-process planning

Each structure reflects a different level of control, flexibility, and coordination. See the full breakdown on our programs page.

How to Make the Right Choice

The most effective way to select a program is through structured case evaluation.

This includes:

  • Reviewing medical background
  • Defining process expectations
  • Aligning objectives with available program structures

A clear recommendation can then be provided based on these factors.

Important Considerations

Program selection should not be based solely on simplified comparisons.

Each case is individual, and selecting the right structure requires understanding how different elements of the process interact.

A properly structured program reduces uncertainty and supports a more controlled journey. For background context, see our main overview and the timeline article.

Conclusion

Choosing the right surrogacy program in Armenia requires understanding both the process and individual case factors.

A structured approach ensures that the selected program aligns with medical needs, process requirements, and overall objectives.
